Condition Overview

What is this condition?

The common cold is a viral infection of your upper respiratory tract — your nose and throat. It's usually harmless, although it might not feel that way. Many types of viruses can cause a common cold.

Key Facts
  • Usually resolves in 7-10 days
  • Caused by viruses (not bacteria)
  • Highly contagious
  • More common in winter months
Symptoms to Watch
Normal Symptoms
• Runny or stuffy nose
• Sore throat
• Mild cough
• Low-grade fever
• Mild body aches
Warning Signs
• High fever (over 101°F)
• Severe headache
• Difficulty breathing
• Chest pain
• Symptoms lasting >10 days

Recommended Medications

Over-the-Counter Medications
Acetaminophen (Tylenol)
Pain/Fever

For headache, fever, and body aches. Take as directed on package.

Dosage: 500-1000mg every 4-6 hours
Ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin)
Pain/Inflammation

Reduces pain, fever, and inflammation. Take with food.

Dosage: 200-400mg every 6-8 hours
Decongestant (Sudafed)
Nasal Congestion

Relieves stuffy nose and sinus pressure. Use for maximum 3-5 days.

Dosage: 30-60mg every 4-6 hours
⚠️ Important Notes
  • • Always follow package instructions
  • • Don't exceed recommended dosages
  • • Consult doctor if taking other medications
  • • Stop use if side effects occur
  • • Not suitable for children under certain ages

Important Precautions

Prevent Spread
Wash Hands Frequently

Use soap and water for at least 20 seconds

Avoid Close Contact

Stay away from others, especially vulnerable individuals

Cover Coughs/Sneezes

Use tissue or elbow, not hands

Disinfect Surfaces

Clean frequently touched objects regularly

Self-Care Measures
Get Plenty of Rest

Sleep 7-9 hours per night to support recovery

Stay Hydrated

Drink 8-10 glasses of water daily

Use Humidifier

Add moisture to air to ease breathing

Gargle Salt Water

Soothe sore throat and reduce inflammation

⚠️ When to Seek Medical Attention
• High fever (over 101°F or 38.3°C)
• Severe headache or stiff neck
• Difficulty breathing or chest pain
• Symptoms lasting more than 10 days
• Worsening symptoms after initial improvement
• Signs of dehydration (dizziness, dry mouth, little urination)
• Underlying health conditions that may complicate recovery

Recovery Timeline

1
Days 1-3: Initial Phase

Symptoms typically peak during this period. Focus on rest and hydration.

What to do:
  • • Complete bed rest
  • • Drink plenty of fluids
  • • Take medications as needed
  • • Monitor symptoms closely
  • • Avoid contact with others
2
Days 4-7: Recovery Phase

Symptoms begin to improve. Gradually increase activity levels.

What to do:
  • • Light walking if feeling better
  • • Continue hydration
  • • Gradual return to normal diet
  • • Gentle stretching exercises
  • • Monitor for any setbacks
3
Days 8-10: Resolution Phase

Most symptoms should be resolved. Return to normal activities gradually.

What to do:
  • • Resume normal activities slowly
  • • Continue good hygiene practices
  • • Maintain healthy diet
  • • Regular exercise routine
  • • Get adequate sleep
4
Day 11+: Full Recovery

Return to complete normalcy with improved immune function.

What to do:
  • • Full return to normal activities
  • • Continue healthy lifestyle habits
  • • Regular exercise program
  • • Maintain good nutrition
  • • Consider immune-boosting supplements
